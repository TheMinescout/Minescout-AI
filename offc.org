                  <p class="has-medium-font-size">
                    But if you haven’t seen enough, scroll down to see some of the other activities our members enjoy by being a part of the Overlake Fly Fishing Club.
                  </p>
                  <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                    Members Meeting
                  </h5>
                  <div class="wp-block-media-text is-stacked-on-mobile" style="grid-template-columns:30% auto">
                    <figure class="wp-block-media-text__media">
                      <img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1024" height="768" src="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/IMG_0654-1024x768.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2429 size-full" srcset="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/IMG_0654-1024x768.jpg 1024w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/IMG_0654-300x225.jpg 300w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/IMG_0654-768x576.jpg 768w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/IMG_0654-800x600.jpg 800w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 1362px) 62vw, 840px" />
                    </figure>
                    <div class="wp-block-media-text__content">
                      <p>
                        The OFFC has has a members monthly meeting, where members can socialize, share fish tales and conduct the business of the club. The meetings often include a diner (optional), socialization time, and a fly fishing presentation. Presentation topics include fly fishing equipment, fishing locations, conservation, techniques, and other topics of interest to fly fishers.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                        Regular Monthly Club Meeting (September-May)
                      </p>
                      <p>
                        Fourth Tuesday of the month, 6:00 – 9:30PM VFW – Redmond
                        <a href="#vfw-map">                        (Map and directions below)
</a>
                        all attendees must show proof of vaccination per VFW and state rules.
                        <br />
                        Typical Agenda:
                        <br />
                        6:30 PM – 7:00 PM Fish Tales  (member driven)
                        <br />
                        7:00 PM – 7:30 Club Business
                        <br />
                        7:30 – 7:40 Break/Buffer
                        <br />
                        7:45 – 8:00 PM   Raffle
                        <br />
                        8:00 PM –  9 PM Guest Speaker
                      </p>
                      <p>
                      </p>
                    </div>
                  </div>
                  <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                    Cub Outings
                  </h5>
                  <div class="wp-block-media-text has-media-on-the-right is-stacked-on-mobile">
                    <div class="wp-block-media-text__content">
                      <p>
                        Outings are a popular club activity. Every year the club host numerous Fly Fishing outings both multi-day destinations trips and
                        <em>                        single day local trips
</em>
                        . Most are no cost to members. Some, especially destination trips, involve a cost.
                      </p>
                    </div>
                    <figure class="wp-block-media-text__media">
                      <img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="720" height="540" src="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Picture11.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-14206 size-full" srcset="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Picture11.jpg 720w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Picture11-300x225.jpg 300w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Picture11-400x300.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 720px) 85vw, 720px" />
                    </figure>
                  </div>
                  <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                    Youth Program
                  </h5>
                  <div class="wp-block-media-text is-stacked-on-mobile">
                    <figure class="wp-block-media-text__media">
                      <img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1024" height="977" src="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001-1024x977.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-14210 size-full" srcset="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001-1024x977.jpg 1024w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001-300x286.jpg 300w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001-768x733.jpg 768w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001-1200x1145.jpg 1200w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/KidsCast15001.jpg 1500w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 1362px) 62vw, 840px" />
                    </figure>
                    <div class="wp-block-media-text__content">
                      <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                        Are Goals are
                      </h5>
                      <p>
                        Provide an environment that encourages youths and their family and/or guardians to learn the skills of Fly Fishing and respect for our fishing environment.
                        <br />
                      </p>
                      <p>
                        Provide youth and family and/or guardians the opportunity to get exposed to many aspects of Fly Fishing and understand the safety issues for each.
                        <br />
                      </p>
                      <p>
                        Provide opportunities to: Understand the equipment; Learn to cast; Learn about bugs and how to Tie flies; Environmental Awareness / Conservation / Fishing Etiquette; Go Fishing Other related fly fishing activities as well as participate in club activities
                      </p>
                    </div>
                  </div>
                  <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                    Conservation
                  </h5>
                  <div class="wp-block-media-text is-stacked-on-mobile">
                    <figure class="wp-block-media-text__media">
                      <img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="871" height="625" src="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/CedarCleanUp1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-14213 size-full" srcset="https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/CedarCleanUp1.jpg 871w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/CedarCleanUp1-300x215.jpg 300w, https://offc.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/CedarCleanUp1-768x551.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 1362px) 62vw, 840px" />
                    </figure>
                    <div class="wp-block-media-text__content">
                      <p>
                        OFFC is committed to conservation especially whenever it concerns fisheries. The club has a conservation line item in the budget to fund conservation projects. The club also participates in the annual Cedar river clean up. Where members can participate in improving this local resource.
                      </p>
                    </div>
                  </div>
                  <h5 class="wp-block-heading">
                    Education
                  </h5>
                  <div class="wp-block-columns is-layout-flex wp-container-core-columns-is-layout-9d6595d7 wp-block-columns-is-layout-flex">
                    <div class="wp-block-column is-layout-flow wp-block-column-is-layout-flow">
                      <p>
                        Many of our members have expertise in various aspects of Fly Fishing. We draw on these members to share there knowledge by offering a wide range
                        <br />
                        of educational programs. Most of these programs are free to members. Scroll down and see some of the programs we have offered in the past.
                      </p>
                      <ul class="wp-block-list">
                        <li>
                          Casting: Beginning-Never ever or limited experience.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Casting: Intermediate/Advanced-Improve loops, accuracy &amp; versatility.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Single Handed: Spey-Spey casts with a single-handed rod.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Fly Tying: Beginning-Learn to use tools &amp; tie a progression of flies.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Fly Tying: Intermediate/Advanced-Tie advanced patterns using new techniques &amp; materials.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Still Water Fishing: Lake tactics, equipment &amp; strategies.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Moving Water Fishing: Reading water &amp; fly line management for moving water.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Rod Building: Build a rod from a graphite blank.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Components Building: Build reel seats &amp; cork handles from raw materials.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Entomology: Learn about bugs in streams that trout eat.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                          Rod, Lines, and Leader Characteristics: Rod performance, effects of lines &amp; leaders, and leader construction.
                        </li>
                      </ul>
                    </div>
                    <div class="wp-block-column is-layout-flow wp-block-column-is-layout-flow">
                      <figure class="wp-block-image size-full">

                      </figure>
